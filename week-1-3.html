Step 1: Define the Style of the Site

Before starting the design process, it's important to define the overall style and aesthetic of the website. Consider factors such as color schemes, typography, and visual elements that align with the desired style.

 

A High-Tech Concept

To align with our Design Engineering subject, our team decided to incorporate a high-tech concept into the visual design of Novva and the website. This choice reflects the technological focus of our team's work and creates a cohesive theme throughout the website.

The high-tech concept can be expressed through various design elements, such as futuristic aesthetics, sleek lines, vibrant colors, and modern typography. By incorporating these elements, our webwebsite can visually convey the innovative and cutting-edge nature of our team's work.

 

Step 2: Define the Sections

Next, determine the sections that will make up the web page. Our website project include the Home Page, Team Members, Work Projects, and Final Project. Clearly defining these sections will help guide the design process and ensure a cohesive layout.

 

Step 3: Start with the Home Page

Begin designing the web page by focusing on the Home Page. This is typically the most important and visually impactful part of the design. Pay attention to creating an attractive and engaging layout that effectively communicates the purpose and message of the website.

Step 4: Create Frames and Name Them

In Figma, start by creating frames for each section of the web page. Use the dimensions of a 1440 wide PC frame as a reference. Naming the frames appropriately will help you stay organized throughout the design process.

Step 5: Create Components

To maintain consistency and efficiency, create components for reusable elements such as headers, footers, buttons, and navigation bars. Components allow you to make changes in one place and have them reflected across the entire design.

Step 6: Use the Layout Tool

Figma provides a layout tool that helps you position and align components within the frames. Utilize this tool to ensure proper spacing, alignment, and overall visual harmony in your design.

Step 7: Repeat the Process

Once you have designed the Home Page, repeat the same process for the remaining sections of the web page, such as Team Members, Work Projects, and Final Project. Maintain a consistent style and layout throughout the entire design.

 

 

By following these steps, you can create a great and professional web page design in Figma. Remember to regularly save your progress and iterate on the design as needed to achieve the desired outcome.
